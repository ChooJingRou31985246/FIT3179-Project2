Caring for environment, caring for Earth

Where does Greenhouse Gases Come From?

73.2% of the greenhouse gases came from energy production. In energy sector such as energy in industry, buildings, transportation and fuel combustion have release most of the greenhouse gases among sub-sectors.

Global Greenhouse Gas Emissions by Gas

In greenhouse gases emissions, carbon dioxide has been release the most to the atmosphere, which is 74.4%.

Who has the Most CO2 Emissions in the World?

By 2019, the world has release 36.44 billion metric tons of CO2 to the air. Lots of the countries have produce large amount of CO2, such as China has the most amount of CO2 emissions in the world. However, China is not the highest CO2 emissions per capita among the world. Mongolia has the most CO2 emissions per capita among the world.

Earth is having a Fever!

The trends of global average temperature keeps growing year by year. It has reached the highest temperature in 2016, which is 0.8℃.
If CO2 and greenhouse gases emissions keeps increasing, it will bring up some unpredictable consequences, such as rises of sea-level and animal extinction. Let’s reduce the CO2 emissions today by doing some small actions such as car-sharing, planting trees and reduce the usage of electrical appliances.
